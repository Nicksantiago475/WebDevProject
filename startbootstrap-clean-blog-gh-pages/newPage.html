Rainy Day Steps

Graph of Average Income by State

This graph shows the average income for each state in the U.S. As we can see, there is a lot of money being made. What happens if it all of our income goes away due to a tragic accident in life? It is key to contribute small amounts of our money to this Rainy Day Fund in order to save up for the future and any unforseen events that may distrupt our daily financial transactions.

Rainy Day Fund Step #1 - Calculate

In order to start creating your own personal Rainy Day Fund, we first need to calculate the amound of money we need to have one. For example, if your monthly expenses are $3,000 and you want to save for three months, your emergency fund goal and amount you need would be $9,000. Here are the three reccomended steps for calculating you amount:

      1. Look at your monthly budget

      2. Remove all non-essentials, things you don't need

      3. Multiply monthly budget by months saving for [3 months or 6 motnhs]

Rainy Day Fund Step #2 - Pick a Bank

After you have found the amount of money you will be trying to save for, it is now time to pick a bank that we can deposit and save our money. We don't want to put our money into the wrong hands so it is important to pick a bank in your area that is the most safest to you. Here are some tips we reccomend when considering what Bank to choose:

      Pick a Bank with:
      FDIC Insurance, Sub accounts, No monthly fess, and Separate logins from checking

      Savings accounts we recommend:
      Capital One 360, Betterment Cash Reserve, and Marcus by Goldman Sachs

Rainy Day Fund Step #3 - Save

Now that you know your amount and have a bank to store your savings, all you have to do is save! This is the most crucial step in creating a Rainy Day Fund. Saving portions of you monthly income will help build your Rainy Day Fund which will make it ready and available whenever you get one of those 'Rainy Days'. Here are some reccomened tips when it comes to saving:

      1. Consistency is key!

      2. Include your savings contributions in monthly budget

      3. Make it a priority!

      4. Do not pull from your rainy day fund unless it is absolutely necessary

    Save as much as you can! You got this!
